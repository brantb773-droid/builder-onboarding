CH.01
wallet

connect your wallet

owockibot checks who you are with a wallet signature, not a password. click connect, pick a wallet, sign the message it shows you. that signature proves you own the wallet. it costs no gas and moves no funds.

what the signature actually does: it's a sign-in message (the eip-4361 standard, aka "sign-in with ethereum"), not a transaction. your wallet will say "sign" or "sign-in request," never "send" or "approve."

before you sign, check the network badge in the wallet popup matches what the bounty board expects. wrong network is the most common reason a "connect" turns into a mystery error.

watch for this signing in never costs gas and never moves funds. if a popup asks you to approve a transaction or spending limit just to log in, close it, you're not on the real page anymore.

status tags to know: open means anyone can claim it, claimed means someone's working it, in review means a submission's already in and being checked.

reward amount and deadline live on the card, but scope, format, and acceptance criteria live in the full brief. that's where most of the useful detail is.

watch for this the card title is a summary, not the spec. skimming it and claiming anyway is the fastest way to submit something that's off-scope and get sent back for a rewrite.
CH.03
lock it in

claim a bounty

hit claim and the bounty locks under your wallet for a set window. it moves into "my claims" and off the open list, so nobody else can grab it while you're working.

bounty detail
agent coordination writeup
120 usdcclaim window: 5 days

the claim window is a courtesy clock, not a hard deadline enforced mid-write. it's there so bounties don't sit locked and untouched while everyone else assumes it's taken.

if you claim something and realize partway through it's not for you, drop it. an open bounty someone else can finish beats a claimed one that quietly expires.

watch for this claim windows expire. check the exact window on the bounty board before you commit, "a few days" here means whatever the timer actually says, not a rough guess.
CH.04
handoff

submit your work

drop a link to the finished piece, add a short note on what you did, and submit. status flips to "in review" and a reviewer takes it from there.

submit form
github.com/you/repo/blob/main/post.html
published to pages, covers coordination patterns as scoped in the brief.

the link has to actually resolve for a reviewer with no context: public repo, published page, not a local draft or a private doc only you can open.

match the format the brief asked for. if it wanted a published page, a raw markdown file in an unmerged branch isn't the same thing, even if the content is done.

watch for this a broken or private link is the single biggest cause of review delays. open your submission link in a private/incognito window before you submit, that's what the reviewer sees.
CH.05
payout

get paid

once a reviewer approves the submission, payout fires on-chain to the wallet you connected back in channel 01. the transaction hash gets logged against the bounty so there's a record either side can check.

if a submission needs changes instead of approval, it bounces back to you with reviewer notes rather than getting rejected outright. fix and resubmit through the same flow.

payout always goes to the wallet on file for that claim. it's set the moment you connect in channel 01, not the moment you submit.

watch for this switching wallets between claiming and getting paid sends the payout to whichever wallet was connected when you claimed, not your current one. stick with one wallet for the whole flow.
field notes

known interference

the five mistakes that show up most across every channel. worth a scan before your first run.

01 · wrong network

signature fails silently

your wallet's on a different network than the board expects. check the network badge before you sign, not after it errors out.

02 · unread brief

scope mismatch

the card title isn't the spec. open the full brief before you claim, not after you've already started writing.

03 · expired claim

timer runs out mid-work

claim windows are real deadlines. if you can't finish in time, drop the claim early instead of letting it lapse.

04 · format mismatch

link doesn't resolve

private repos, local drafts, unpublished pages. test your submission link in an incognito tab before you send it.

05 · wallet switch

payout to the wrong address

payout follows the wallet connected at claim time. don't swap wallets mid-flow unless you're prepared to lose the payout to the old one.

06 · gas confusion

paying for a free step

connecting and signing in cost no gas. if your wallet's asking you to pay for either, something's off, stop and double check the site.

frequency check

faq

do i need eth or gas to connect and sign in?

no. connecting your wallet and signing the verification message are both free. gas only enters the picture if a bounty explicitly involves an on-chain transaction as part of the deliverable, which the brief will say up front.

can i claim more than one bounty at once?

check the bounty board itself for your current claim limit, it's shown per-account and can change. treat it as the source of truth over anything written here.

what happens if i miss my claim deadline?

the bounty reopens and goes back to "open" status for anyone to claim. no penalty beyond losing your spot, so it's worth dropping a claim early if you know you won't finish.

how long does payout take after a submission's approved?

payout fires once a reviewer marks the submission approved. exact timing depends on the review cycle for that bounty, check the board or ask in the builder channel if it's been a while.

i don't have a wallet yet. where do i start?

any standard wallet that supports message signing works, metamask and coinbase wallet are the most common starting points. install the wallet, create or import an account, then come back to channel 01.

my submission got sent back. did i lose the bounty?

no. "changes requested" isn't a rejection, it's reviewer notes. fix what's flagged and resubmit through the same claim, you don't need to reclaim it.

where do i ask questions that aren't covered here?

drop into the builder community channel linked in the footer below. that's also the fastest way to flag a bug in the flow itself.
